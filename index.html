My Background and Ambitions

I was born and raised in Seoul, Korea. Seoul is the capital city of Seoul and is one of the most densely populated cities in the world ( according to wikipedia there are 25,620,000 people living in the Metropolitan area as of October, 2014). I have lived in Epsom, Surrey, London for about four years, where I attended primary school and high school. After finishing high school in Seoul, I enrolled in Hongik University School of Architecture. My dream was to become an architect since I was twelve. After finishing two years of my undergraduate study, I joined the military (like all healthy men in Korea do) and served my nation for two years. Then before returning to school, I went backpacking to Spain by myself and travelled through the whole country as well as Portu and Lisbon of Portugal. I had travelled most of Europe as well as Egypt and other asian countries with my family. But travelling alone was a very special experience.After studying Architecture for a year and a half more in Seoul, I decided to transfer to IIT to finish my Bachelor's here, as a dual degree program.

Since coming to IIT, I had my rough first semester here. Trying to convert from the Metric system to the Imperial system was a bigger problem than I had thought - the parts of the models I was making wouldn't fit because the measurements were ever-so-slightly off. Then all the materials were different too. But I was learning a lot and I knew that is what mattered most. I received shitty grades, but decided not to beat myself up over it. It was part of trial and error.

Now I am pursuing my dream as I always had been - only at a larger scale. I have applied for internship at London this Summer, and Study abroad program in Netherlands for the Fall semester. I am trying to get out in the world - to refine my skills and experience the various Architecture scenes of the world I had always dreamt of. My ambition lies in establishing my own practice and school of Architecture at a international scale. It's not that I am after wealth or fame, but I wish to master my own Architecture. My dream is to design and build a masterpiece that will live on even after my death. Incorporating technology into Architecture is one of concepts that I am pursuing.

Taking this course will play a big role in my professional career. At some point in my life, I had to build a website for myself. For Architects, this has become almost a basic requirement, where all the firms require a website address from the applicants. And of course, if I were to establish my own practice, it is an absolute necessity. I had been putting it off. I kept telling myself that I was too busy with Studio work and Competitions to learn how to do all of this. There was always a lot of work to be done. But I realised I had to do this NOW. It was time.

I am very excited to be learning how to build a site - from the ground up. This BIOS-like screen is really throwing me off, and half of the time I have no idea what is going on. Like that Facebook meme - when a code doesn't work, I don't know why, and when it works, I don't know why. But it is very entertaining to explore this new world, especially with a doctor helping me out on Campfire like a chat-pal. Maybe after some time, I will start to understand those articles on the forums. And by the end of this course, perhaps I will have a good idea about how I will design my own site - Get out in the world and reach out.
